Survey Results Analysis

The team conducted a survey to determine if the proposed project satisfies a need on the UP Campus. The survey was conducted with 53 respondents, including students from different semesters. Below is the summary of the findings:

  • Question: ¿De qué semestre eres?
    Findings: The respondents were from various semesters, ranging from first to ninth.
  • Question: ¿Qué tan difícil te ha resultado encontrar tus salones de clase en la Universidad?
    Findings: Most respondents indicated a medium to high difficulty in finding their classrooms.
  • Question: ¿Crees que un video explicativo te sería útil para encontrar tus salones de clase?
    Findings: A significant majority agreed that an explanatory video would be useful.
  • Question: ¿Conoces los nombres y ubicaciones de los edificios y aulas dentro del campus?
    Findings: The responses were divided; while some know the buildings partially, others do not.
  • Question: Del 1 al 5, ¿qué tan probable es que uses un código QR en el campus para ver un video explicativo?
    Findings: There was a varied distribution, but the majority leaned towards a high likelihood of using QR codes if available.
  • Question: ¿En qué lugares del campus te gustaría que se colocaran los códigos QR?
    Findings: The preferred locations for QR codes included entrances, common areas, cafeterias, and other strategic points around the campus.

Conclusions

Based on the survey findings, it is evident that there is a significant need for better navigation aids on the campus. Most students expressed difficulty in locating their classrooms and agreed that a video guide could be beneficial. The idea of using QR codes to provide information was well-received, with students suggesting their placement in high-traffic and strategic areas.

These results confirm that the proposed project could address a significant problem on the UP Campus and that there is a clear demand for its implementation.
